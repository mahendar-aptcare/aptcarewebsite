Care Delivery

Read More
Care delivery involves providing healthcare services to individuals, emphasizing prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and support. It encompasses a continuum of care settings, from primary care clinics to hospitals and community services. Effective care delivery ensures accessibility, quality, and patient-centered approaches for promoting overall health and well-being.

Pre-Hospital Care

Read More
Pre-hospital care involves immediate medical assistance provided before a patient reaches a hospital. It includes recognizing emergencies, ensuring scene safety, assessing patients, and administering basic or advanced life support.

Inpatient (In-Hospital) Care

Read More
Inpatient care occurs within a hospital or healthcare facility, where patients receive comprehensive medical treatment and monitoring. Healthcare professionals address acute conditions, perform surgeries, and manage recovery. Patients stay overnight or longer, benefiting from continuous medical attention, diagnostic tests, therapeutic interventions, and multidisciplinary healthcare teams for optimal care.

Post-Hospital Care

Read More
Post-hospital care, or post-acute care, extends medical support after a hospital stay. It focuses on recovery, rehabilitation, and managing chronic conditions. Services may include home health care, skilled nursing facilities, or rehabilitation centers. The goal is to enhance patients' well-being, independence, and quality of life following hospitalization.
